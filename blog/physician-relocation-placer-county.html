Why Physicians Are Moving to the Sacramento Region

The Greater Sacramento area has seen a significant influx of physicians over the past several years, driven largely by the expansion of major health systems in the region. UC Davis Medical Center is one of the leading academic medical centers on the West Coast and consistently draws physicians, residents, and fellows from across the country. Sutter Health, Kaiser Permanente, and Dignity Health all have a strong regional presence with multiple facilities throughout Sacramento and Placer County.

For physicians coming from the Bay Area, Sacramento offers something increasingly rare — the ability to actually afford a home on a physician's income. The price-to-income ratio here is dramatically more favorable, and the quality of life in communities like Rocklin, Roseville, and Granite Bay is genuinely exceptional.

Placer County vs. Sacramento: Which Side Makes Sense?

This is one of the first questions I work through with every physician client, because the answer depends heavily on where you will be working and what matters most to your family.

If you are working at UC Davis Medical Center, the campus sits in Sacramento near Highway 50. From Rocklin or Roseville, you are typically looking at a 30 to 45 minute commute depending on traffic. Many physicians make this drive daily and find it manageable — especially given what they get in return: newer construction, excellent schools, lower crime rates, and significantly more space for the money.

If you are working at Sutter Roseville or a Placer County facility, living in Rocklin or Roseville puts you five to fifteen minutes from work. This is a very different lifestyle and worth serious consideration if you have young children or irregular shift schedules.

Many physicians I work with initially assume they need to live close to Sacramento and end up in Placer County anyway once they visit and see what the communities feel like. Rocklin and Roseville in particular have a quality of life that is hard to convey on paper.

The Neighborhoods Physicians Most Often Choose

Granite Bay is the prestige address in Placer County. Homes here range from the high $700s to well over $3 million for lakefront or custom estates. My highest residential sale — $2.24 million on Shelborne Drive — was in Granite Bay. The public schools feed into the highly rated Granite Bay High School. If you have school-age children and a higher budget, this is frequently the first neighborhood I show physician clients.

Rocklin sits at a sweet spot of price, quality, and community. Median home prices hover around $700,000 to $850,000. I am based in Rocklin and know this market street by street.

Roseville is the largest city in Placer County and offers the most variety in price points and neighborhoods. Sutter Roseville Medical Center is located here, which makes it a natural choice for physicians joining that system.

Lincoln offers more land, newer construction at lower price points, and a quieter lifestyle. For physicians working at facilities in north Placer County, it can be an excellent fit.

What the Timeline Actually Looks Like

Most physicians are finalizing employment contracts, dealing with credentialing, managing licensing in a new state, and trying to move a family — all simultaneously. The home search often gets pushed to the last possible moment. Here is what I typically recommend:

  • Six months out: Start researching neighborhoods online. Visit for a weekend if possible and drive through Rocklin, Roseville, and Granite Bay.
  • Three to four months out: Connect with a lender and get pre-approved. Physician loan programs are a significant advantage here.
  • Two to three months out: Begin actively searching. Well-priced homes in desirable areas still move quickly — being pre-approved and ready gives you a real advantage.
  • Six weeks out: Ideally you are under contract by this point, giving time for inspections, appraisal, and closing before your start date.

I have helped physician clients compress this timeline significantly when circumstances required it. It is doable, but the more lead time you have, the better the outcome.

Physician Loan Programs: A Quick Note

Many physicians I work with are not aware of how favorable mortgage programs can be for their profession. Because physician income is highly predictable and physician default rates are very low, many lenders have created dedicated physician loan products that allow for:

  • Zero to 5% down payment with no private mortgage insurance (PMI)
  • Qualification based on an employment contract before your start date
  • Student loan debt treated more favorably in debt-to-income calculations

I work with several lenders in the Sacramento area who specialize in physician financing and can make introductions at no cost to you.
